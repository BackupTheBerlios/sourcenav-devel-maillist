On Tue, Jan 27, 2009 at 4:41 AM, Freek <freequaos at gmail.com> wrote:

> Hi Ryan,
>
> Daniel explained it correctly already. The reason for the real fork
> and that we are on Berlios is that the "old" SN guys were a bit
> hesisant to hand over the project page so I could start the SVN tree
> and get things started.
> The reason behind SN 6.0 (which is EXACTLY SN NG3, only with a changed
> version string) is that we finally got  the SN SourceForge project
> from the original SN guys.
> We felt that still a lot of people were looking at and using the
> original SN and not using the enhanced NG version, so we made the 6.0
> to spread the word that we are still alive and all further development
> is done by the NG project.
>
> The original reason for restarting the development was that I go
> frustrated with SN's flakyness and non-friendly look (the pixmaps they
> used just carry that ancient Unix look that hurts your eyes).
> It also used the old db-1.85 which had memory corruption issues for
> really big projects. And the only reason why you'd use SN ARE big
> projects you have to explore quickly (I cannot consider cscope or
> similar stuff to be a solution to that particular problem - Source
> Insight is rather nice, though).
> So we took off to fix some long-standing bugs, rework the look, and
> eventually change over to a recent db4 database library (where we
> luckily had big contributions).
> Mark quickly joined in the very beginning (at first contributing
> enhanced ClearCase support) and after NG3 Maurice joined, too.
>
> Our focus remains to improve usability and performance. We are also
> trying to stay as cross-platform as we can, although we fail from time
> to time ;-) SN NG should run on major Unixes (w/ gcc at least), win32
> (Msys/minGW) and OS X (Mark is doing the tweaking there). Our primary
> platform stays Linux, though.
>
> Hope this helps, cheers and happy hacking,
