Hi Freek,

I think I have something...

- I figured out that the light blue bgcolor of include.xpm is '#7aa0d0'
- I figured out  that the green bgcolor of tilda_left.xpm is '#71BF70'
- I used convert(1) to replace green with blue as in

convert  -fill '#7aa0d0' -opaque '#71bf70'  tilda_left.xpm
new_tilda_left.xpm

convert(1) is part of ImageMagick RPM.

The ImageMagic package  has a command called display(1). You can use this or
gimp(1) to figure out the color RGB code...the rest is done by above
convert(1).

1- bash $>  display include.xpm
2- A tiny icon is displayed, click on it, a menu is displayed
3- Navigate to  Image Edit > Color ...
4- Click on Pixel Color , a color selection pane is displayed
5- Click on Grab, click on a region of an image, the color code is displayed
