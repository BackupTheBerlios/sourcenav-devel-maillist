And why is the tab stop default 8?  IIRC, it is because IBM used the  
first 8 columns of a punch card for a card sequence number and the tab  
key got you to the coding column.  That default carried into the line  
editors and then into the graphical editors.  Eight characters of  
white space is a waste of pixel environment.  Programming languages  
with length syntax and programs with lengthy variable names have  
caused programmers to reduce the white space to make the code more  
readable.
